Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun and known as the "Red Planet" due to iron oxide rust, is a cold, rocky desert with a thin CO
 atmosphere. Roughly half the size of Earth, it features massive volcanoes, deep canyons, and signs of past liquid water. NASA and ESA actively explore Mars with rovers, searching for past life and preparing for potential future human habitation.

Key Aspects of Mars
Physical Features: Mars has a dense core made of iron, nickel, and sulfur, surrounded by a rocky mantle and a crust, making it a terrestrial planet similar to Earth. It is home to Olympus Mons, the largest volcano in the solar system, which is about three times the height of Mount Everest.
Climate & Atmosphere: The atmosphere is thin (mostly carbon dioxide, nitrogen, and argon), leading to extreme temperatures ranging from -153°C to 20°C (-243°F to 68°F).
Exploration: Currently, multiple robots, including NASA's Curiosity and Perseverance rovers, explore the surface. Scientists study Mars to understand its evolution, search for evidence of ancient life, and prepare for human exploration.
Time & Size: A day on Mars (24.6 hours) is similar to Earth's, but its year lasts 687 Earth days. It is approximately half the size of Earth, with a surface area equal to Earth's dry land.
Moons: Mars has two small, irregularly shaped moons, Phobos and Deimos.

Current Mars Missions
Perseverance Rover: The rover is investigating Jezero Crater, studying rock samples and looking for signs of ancient life.
Curiosity Rover: As of August 2025, it has been exploring the planet for 13 years.
ESA ExoMars: This mission aims to drill into the surface to look for signs of life.

Why Study Mars?
Search for Life: Mars is considered the most habitable planet for potential past or present life.
Climate Studies: Researchers want to know how Mars transitioned from a warmer, wetter planet to a barren desert.
Future Colonization: Due to its proximity and accessible water (underground ice), it is a primary candidate for human exploration and potential colonization.
